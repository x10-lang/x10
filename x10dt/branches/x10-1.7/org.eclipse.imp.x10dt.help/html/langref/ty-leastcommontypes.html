9  Least common ancestor of types

To compute the type of conditional expressions (), and of rail constructors (), the least common ancestor of types must be computed.
The least common ancestor of two types "T1" and "T2" is the unique most-specific type that is a supertype of both "T1" and "T2".
If the most-specific type is not unique (which can happen when "T1" and "T2" both implement two or more incomparable interfaces), then least common ancestor type is "x10.lang.Object".
